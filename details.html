Duck leg steak

Duck leg steak is quick and easy to make with just a handful of everyday ingredients, and is perfect for warm weather picnics, potlucks, lunches, or light dinners

Ingredients

  • 2 duck legs (skin-on, bone-in)
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic (crushed)
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme or rosemary
  • 1 tbsp olive oil or duck fat
  • Lemon juice
  • 1 tsp lemon juice or balsamic vinegar (optional)
  • 1 tsp honey or soy sauce (optional for glaze)

Steps

  1. Prep the duck legs Pat dry with paper towels. Score the skin in a crisscross pattern (don’t cut into the meat). Season generously with salt and pepper.
  2. Sear skin side down Heat a pan over medium heat. Place duck legs skin-side down (no oil needed if skin is fatty). Cook 6–8 minutes until skin is crispy and golden.
  3. Flip and add flavor Turn legs over. Add olive oil or duck fat, garlic, and herbs. Cook 4–5 minutes to brown the meat side.
  4. Optional glaze For a sweet touch, brush with honey or soy sauce in the last minute of cooking.
  5. Finish in oven (if needed) For thicker legs, transfer to a 180°C (350°F) oven for 10–15 minutes to cook through.
  6. Rest and serve Let rest 5 minutes. Drizzle with lemon juice or balsamic vinegar for brightness.
  7. Enjoy!
